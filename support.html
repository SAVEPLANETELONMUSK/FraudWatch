Support Centre

Being affected by fraud can impact your confidence, finances, and emotional wellbeing. You are not alone. FraudWatch is here to provide practical guidance, educational resources, and support to help you move forward.

🤝 You're Not Alone

Millions of people around the world experience scams every year. Fraud can affect anyone, regardless of age, education, or experience. Reaching out for support is a positive step toward recovery.

🧠 Emotional Recovery

It's common to feel shocked, embarrassed, angry, or anxious after being scammed. Give yourself time to recover emotionally and remember that many victims experience similar feelings.

If you're struggling emotionally, consider speaking with someone you trust or seeking support from a qualified mental health professional in your area.

🛡 Stay Safe

Strengthen your online security by using strong passwords, enabling two-factor authentication, keeping your devices updated, and verifying identities before sending money or sharing sensitive information.

📚 Learn About Scams

Knowledge is one of the strongest defenses against fraud. Visit our Education Centre to learn how common scams work, recognize warning signs, and understand how to protect yourself.

📋 Recovery Plan

If you believe you've been affected by fraud, follow our Recovery Centre checklist to secure your accounts, preserve important evidence, and understand practical next steps.

🚨 Report Fraud

Reporting suspected scams can help raise awareness and support efforts to reduce fraud. If you have information about a suspected scam, consider submitting a report through FraudWatch and, where appropriate, to the relevant authorities in your country.

Report a Scam

🛡 Safety Reminder

Always verify a person's identity before sending money or sharing sensitive information. Be cautious of urgent payment requests, pressure to act quickly, or requests to keep financial transactions secret. Taking a few moments to verify information can significantly reduce the risk of fraud.

Verify Before You Trust

❤️ Recovery Takes Time

Recovering after fraud is often a gradual process. Be patient with yourself, focus on practical steps to improve your security, and remember that asking for help is a sign of strength—not weakness.
